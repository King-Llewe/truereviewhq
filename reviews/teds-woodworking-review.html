The Product

What Is Ted's Woodworking?

Ted's Woodworking — created by Ted McGrath, a certified master woodworker and member of the AWI (Architectural Woodwork Institute) — is a digital collection of woodworking plans, video tutorials, and supplementary guides. It has been sold online for over a decade and remains one of the best-known woodworking resources in the DIY digital product space.

The package is delivered entirely digitally: after purchase, you receive access to download everything — the plans are organized into categories and come in PDF format, many with step-by-step instructions, cut lists, materials lists, and dimensioned drawings.

Who Created It? Ted McGrath has 40+ years of woodworking experience and holds professional credentials from the AWI. He spent years compiling this library with the explicit goal of making professional-grade plans accessible to hobbyists without the typical $200–$500 per-plan cost of custom architectural drawings.

Honest Assessment

Plan Quality — The Real Story

We're not going to pretend all 16,000 plans are equally polished. Here's an honest breakdown of what we found:

The Good

The majority of plans — especially in the furniture, outdoor, and workshop categories — are genuinely well-constructed. They include dimensioned drawings, cut lists (materials with exact dimensions), and sequential build instructions. Many of the more complex projects (decks, kitchen cabinets) are detailed enough that you could take them to a lumber yard and get accurate material quotes.

The Variable

Some plans in the "bulk" categories (small projects, decorative items) are simpler — closer to a project sketch than a professional blueprint. They're still usable, but experienced woodworkers may want to supplement them with their own measurements.

Our Recommendation

Use the more detailed plans as-is. For simpler projects, treat them as starting points and adapt to your specific space and materials. Even at this value, it's still dramatically cheaper than buying individual plans elsewhere.
